The Wave Speech

Strange memories on this nervous night...

When? Will it come again?

Maybe it meant something. Maybe not.

But no mix of words will ever convey it.

History is hard to know

Even without being sure . . . the energy comes to head in a long flash, for reasons no one understands...

My central memory

...when I left Filmore half-crazy...

instead of going home, aimed across the Bay Bridge a hundred miles an hour...

... L.L. Bean shorts and a Butte sheepherder's jacket...

...booming through the Treasure Island Tunnel...

... not quite sure which turn-off to take...

always stalling at the toll-gate...

but being absolutely certain that no matter which way I went I would come to a place where people were just as high and wild as I was.

No doubt at all about that...

There was madness in every direction, at any hour.

If not across the Bay, then up the Golden Gate...

You could see sparks anywhere.

There was a fantastic universal sense . . . that we were winning.

Here's the speech clincher...!

And the wave... breaks...

And that, I think was the handle.

That sense of inevitable victory over the forces of Old and Evil.

There was no point in fighting.

We had all the momentum...

We were riding the crest of a high and beautiful wave...

So now, less than five years later... with the right kind of eyes, you can almost see the high-water mark... that place where the wave finally broke and rolled back.
